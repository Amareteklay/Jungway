Resources about Jungian analysis

Depanding on whether you want to go deep into the nitty-gritty of analytical psychology or only go through the most accessible and practical concepts, there are many resources you can leverage. These include Jung's own works including his collected works in 20 volumes or so and his interview videos, books written by other Jungian scholars, talks and podcast shows and many more.

Books

Jung's own books:

  • Memories, Dreams, Reflections (1961)
  • Man and His Symbols (1964)
  • The Undiscovered Self (1961)
  • Dreams (1974)
  • Psychology of the Unconscious (1912)
  • Modern Man in Search of a Soul (1931)
  • The Archetypes and the Collective Unconscious (1959)
  • The Red Book: Liber Novus (2009)
  • Psychology and Alchemy (1944)
  • Symbols of Transformation (1912)

Books by Jungian Scholars:

  • Jung: A Very Short Introduction (Anthony Stevens, 2001)
  • Ego and Archetype: Individuation and the Religious Function of the Psyche (Edward F. Edinger, 1972)
  • Owning Your Own Shadow: Understanding the Dark Side of the Psyche (Robert A. Johnson, 1991)
  • The Middle Passage: From Misery to Meaning in Midlife (James Hollis, 1993)
  • Swamplands of the Soul: New Life in Dismal Places (James Hollis, 1996)
  • Four Pillars of Jungian Psychoanalysis (Murray Stein, 2022)
  • Eden Project: In Search of the Magical Other (James Hollis, 1998)
  • The Portable Jung (Edited by Joseph Campbell, 1976)
  • Modern Woman in Search of Soul: A Jungian Guide to the Visible and Invisible Worlds (June Singer, 1998)
  • Memoir of Tina Keller-Jenny: A Lifelong Confrontation with the Psychology of C.G. Jung (Wendy Swan, 2009)

Training centers

ISAPZURICH offers both full-time and part-time training in Jungian Psychology to help people become Jungian Analysts.

CG Jung Institute of Chicago provides three training programs; two for licensed clinicians (ATP & JPP) and one for those interested in personal growth (JSP).

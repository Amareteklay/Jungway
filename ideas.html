Key Ideas in Depth Psychology (Jungian Analysis)

Jung founded analytical psychology. Common related terms are depth psychology and Jungian analysis. The key features of this approach are that the human psyche has both the conscious and unconscious elements and that by integrating the two a balanced psychological condition can be achieved. The fact that the unconscious is unconscious makes it difficult if not impossible to deal with it. Jungian analysis, however, uses symbols and feeling functions to tap into the message that the unconscious tried to send. The goal is what Jung called individuation, the full realization of one's potential. One should know some key terminology to understand Jungian approach (the Jungway) and apply it to one's life. Many Jungian analysts propose that one live the questions instead of the answers in so far as one is committed to exploring what this thing called life is all about. The following are key concepts and ideas in Jungian analysis. Have them for the journey ahead.

  • Individuation:
  • Persona
  • Shadow
  • The Self
  • Dreams
  • Archetypes
  • Anima, animus
  • Collective unconscious

Carl Gustav Jung (1875-1961)

Carl Gustav Jung was a Swiss psychiatrist and psychoanalyst. He is the father of analytical psychology. Jung was born on 26 July 1875 and died at the age of 86 on 6 June 1961. In 1903 he married Emma Rauschenbach, and they couple had five children.

The influence of Jung's work is not limited to psychology. It permeates the fields of:

  • Psychiatry
  • Anthropology and Archaeology
  • Philosophy and Literature
  • And even religious studies

Key Ideas in Jugian Psychology

Jungian psychology is also referred to as analytical psychology, or Jungian analysis, or depth psychology. Jung believed that the psyche has conscious and unconscious parts and integrating the two is key to a balanced psychological condition.

Murray Stein outlines the following "four pillars of Jungian psychoanalysis," which render Jungian analysis unique.

  • Individuation
  • Analytical relationship
  • Working with dreams
  • Active imagination

Carl Gustav Jung (1875-1961)

Carl Gustav Jung was a Swiss psychiatrist and psychoanalyst who founded and can be thought of as the father of analytical psychology. He was born on 26 July 1875 and died at the age of 86 on 6 June 1961. The influence of Jung's work is not limited to psychology. It permeates the fields of psychiatry, anthropology, archaeology, literature, philosophy, and even religious studies.

Key Ideas in Jugian Psychology

Jung founded analytical psychology. Common related terms are depth psychology and Jungian analysis. The key features of this approach are that the human psyche has both the conscious and unconscious elements and that by integrating the two a balanced psychological condition can be achieved. The fact that the unconscious is unconscious makes it difficult if not impossible to deal with it. Jungian analysis, however, uses symbols and feeling functions to tap into the message that the unconscious tried to send.
